Personal Health Twin (Blockchain + ML) — Abstract
We propose Personal Health Twin, a privacy-preserving, scalable digital replica of an individual’s longitudinal health profile that combines machine learning with blockchain-enabled provenance. The system ingests multi-modal personal data (wearables, EHR snapshots, genomic metadata, lifestyle and environmental sensors) to construct a dynamic, individualized health twin that models physiological baselines and predicts short- and long-term health trajectories. Lightweight, on-device models provide continuous personalization and local inference while federated learning aggregates improvements across users without sharing raw data. A permissioned blockchain records tamper-evident provenance for model updates, consent transactions, and data-access events—ensuring auditability, fine-grained consent, and verifiable lineage for clinical and research use. The platform offers explainable outputs (counterfactuals and feature attributions) to support actionable recommendations for prevention, early-warning detection of anomalies, and personalized intervention scheduling. We evaluate the approach on synthetic and real-world dataset slices for tasks such as early detection of cardio-metabolic risk and medication-adherence forecasting, demonstrating improvements in predictive accuracy, robustness to distribution shift, and user-level privacy compared with centralized baselines. The architecture balances clinical utility, regulatory compliance, and user agency—positioning the Personal Health Twin as a practical bridge between continuous consumer sensing and clinically trustworthy decision support.

AI-Powered E-Waste Image Classification System Using EfficientNetV2B0 with Gradio and Virtual Reality Integration
Abstract:
This project introduces an AI-driven E-Waste Image Classification System that leverages EfficientNetV2B0 (Transfer Learning) to accurately identify and classify e-waste items such as mobile phones, PCBs, batteries, and keyboards, achieving over 90% validation accuracy. The system is enhanced with Gradio, OpenCV, and MediaPipe for multi-modal user interaction through typing, image upload, webcam, IP webcam, and VR gesture control modes, ensuring accessibility and real-time performance. It also integrates cost estimation, recycling recommendations, and dynamic dashboards with Google Maps links to nearby recycling centers, promoting eco-friendly practices. Developed using Python, TensorFlow/Keras, Gradio, OpenCV, and MediaPipe, this project bridges deep learning and sustainable technology, showcasing how artificial intelligence can drive green innovation and support smart city sustainability initiatives.
